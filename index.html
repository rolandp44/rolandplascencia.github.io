About Me

Roland Plascencia

My Philosophy

In my senior year, I played on an undersized football team. I realized quickly that leadership isn’t about barking orders; it’s about creating an environment where everyone feels supported. When I saw the underclassmen struggling, I didn’t just give them the whatI gave them the why.

I went back to the fundamentals, explaining the reasons behind every movement. Even when tensions rose between players, I stepped in to defuse situations individually, prioritizing unity over ego. By keeping the atmosphere light with humor but firm with expectations, I watched a struggling group become the most unified unit on the team.

To me, leadership is about building bonds through empathy, consistency, and trust.

Resilience

The most defining parts of my life have come from challenges that forced me to restart. At 14, I was separated from my parents, losing the stability I had known my entire life. I entered high school lost, using football as my only anchor.

While football taught me how to take hits and keep pushing, I eventually realized I was using it to mask deeper emotional struggles. When I hit a low point in my junior year, I had to stop and rethink who I was outside of the sport.

What I Learned From the Rebuild

Academic Drive

I recognized that my past grades reflected survival mode, not my potential. I am now committed to bringing the same work ethic I had on the field into the classroom.

Emotional Resilience

Spending time in mindfulness taught me how to balance my life and reconnect with my original passions.

Looking Forward

I am more than just a student or an athlete. I am someone who lost their identity and had the strength to rebuild it. Whether I am leading a team or tackling a new academic challenge, I bring a perspective shaped by resilience and a drive to create things that bring people joy.
