About me - Roland Plascencia

My Philosophy

In my senior year, I played on an undersized football team. I realized quickly that leadership isn’t about barking orders; it’s about creating an environment where everyone feels supported. When I saw the underclassmen struggling, I didn't just give them the "what" I gave them the "why." I went back to the fundamentals, explaining the reasons behind every movement. Even when tensions rose between players, I stepped in to defuse the situation individually, prioritizing the unity of the group over ego. By keeping the atmosphere light with humor but stern with expectations, I watched a struggling group become the most unified unit on the team. To me, leadership is building a bond with empathy, consistency, and trust.

Resilience

The most defining parts of my life have come from the challenges that forced me to restart. At 14, I was separated from my parents, losing the stability I had known my entire life. I entered high school lost, and for a long time, I used football as my only anchor. While football taught me how to take hits and keep pushing, I eventually realized I was using it to mask deeper emotional struggles. When I hit a low point in my junior year, I had to stop and rethink who I was outside of the sport.
